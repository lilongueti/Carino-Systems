How to choose an Operating System

Keep in mind your use case, then know your technical limits and then project your future needs.

Microsoft Windows

The brainchild of Microsoft, is like a bustling city that never sleeps. From its humble beginnings as a 16-bit command-line operating system in 1981, it has grown into a vibrant metropolis with the latest release, Windows 11. It’s the city that most of the world’s PCs call home.

macOS

macOS is like a sleek, modern art gallery. Developed by Apple Inc., it’s the curator of Apple’s Mac computers, ensuring every application and file is displayed just right. From its debut in 2001 to the latest exhibit, macOS 14 Sonoma, macOS continues to push the boundaries of design and functionality.

GNU/Linux

Linux is like a bustling bazaar full of diverse stalls (or distros). It’s free, open-source, and there’s something for everyone. Whether you’re looking for something user-friendly like Ubuntu or something more hands-on like Arch Linux, Linux has got you covered.

BSD

BSD, short for “Berkeley Software Distribution”, is like an ancient library filled with wisdom from the University of California, Berkeley. It’s the foundation for many open-source operating systems and continues to inspire with its robust and efficient design.

Not sure about choosing a different system?

You don't have to. An operating system is a tool, as long as you can do what you need with such tool, you don't need to change it. If you don't feel prepared for it, you can always setup a Virtual Machine or a container. Also, on Linux systems you can use a live session with no need to install the system.
